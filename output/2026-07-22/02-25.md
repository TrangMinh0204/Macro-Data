Title: France’s emergency agriculture bill passed, amid divisions over pesticides

France’s National Assembly has approved an emergency agriculture bill after a tense overnight debate, clearing the way for the possible temporary return of two pesticides banned in the country but still authorised elsewhere in the European Union.
French MPs passed the legislation by 296 votes to 224 in the early hours of Tuesday morning, following a fractious sitting that exposed sharp differences between the government and some members of its own parliamentary camp.
The bill now requires a final confirmatory vote in the senate, expected later on Tuesday, before it can be definitively adopted by parliament. Approval in the upper house is considered highly likely, as one of the senate’s main demands – the provision allowing limited pesticide derogations – survived negotiations between the two chambers.
Under the legislation, France’s food, environmental and occupational health agency, [ANSES](https://www.anses.fr/en), could exceptionally authorise the use of acetamiprid and flupyradifurone in agricultural sectors facing serious difficulties.
Both substances are prohibited in France but permitted under [EU rules](https://food.ec.europa.eu/plants/pesticides/maximum-residue-levels/eu-legislation-mrls_en). Any authorisation would be temporary, conditional and restricted to a small number of crops or sectors.
Supporters argue that the measure would help French producers compete with farmers elsewhere in Europe who are allowed to use the same products. Opponents say it risks reversing environmental protections and exposing people and ecosystems to potentially harmful chemicals.
